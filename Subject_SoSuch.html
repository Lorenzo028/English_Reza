Learn The Subjects

So | Such

Definitions

So, Such and Too have the same meaning in sentences, namely "very". Well, how do you use So, Such, and Too in sentences? Let's check it out!

A.So ... That
"So" means "very" and is used as emphasis in a sentence. Here's a pattern that can be used:
... + so + (Adjective/Adverb) + that + ...
After the word "that", you can use a clause like subject + verb + object . “So” can also be used with “many…that” and “much…that”.
Example:
> These boxes are so heavy that we can’t lift them.
> There were so many people in the auditorium that we could barely get in the front door.


B.Such ... That
The meaning of "very" also has the word "Such" and its use aims to explain the emphasis on something. As for the pattern structure:
... + such + (a/an) + (Adjective/Adverb) + Noun Phrase + that + ...
Example:
> It was such a pretty view that he took a photograph..
> You has such a nice car that make me want to buy it.


Quizz
1.The weather was ... hot
2.It was ... a hot day ... we stayed indoors
3.They are ... friendly people.
4.The internet's ... slow this morning
5.There was ... a crowd at the concert
6.There is no ... person!
7.It took ... long to get home last night.
8.There were ... many people on the train ... i had to stand the whole way.
9.It's ... expensive ... we're not going.
10.It cost ... much!

Answers:
1.The weather was so hot.
2.It was such a hot day that we stayed indoors.
3.They are so friendly people.
4.The internet's so slow this morning.
5.There was such a crowd at the concert
6.There is no such person.
7.It took so long to get home last night.
8.There were so many people on the train that i had to stand the whole way.
9.It's so expensive that we're not going.
10.It's cost so much.
